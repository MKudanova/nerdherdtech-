PHYSICAL AND HARDWARE ACCESS POLICY

for WWW.NERDHEARDTECH.COM

Welcome to the official access policy for NerdHerd Technologies Inc. This page outlines our rules for physical entry, hardware access, and use of removable media in a way that keeps our workplace secure, efficient, and—let’s be honest—nerdy.

Introduction

The purpose of this policy is to protect NerdHerd Technologies’ physical assets, sensitive data, and infrastructure from unauthorized access, theft, or harm. Physical security is just as important as digital security, and together, they form a strong defense against modern threats.

Office Suite Access Policy

  • Business Hours: Monday–Friday, 9:00am–5:00pm. Receptionist on duty during these hours.
  • Entry: Employees must use their assigned access badges. Visitors check in at reception and receive a visitor badge.
  • After-Hours Access: Only approved staff with proper authorization. All after-hours entries are logged and monitored by cameras.
  • Security Tech: Electronic card readers, security cameras, and alarm systems protect all entry points.
  • Policy Reminders: No “tailgating.” Always display badges. Report lost badges immediately.

Data Center Access Policy

  • Authorized Personnel Only: Access limited to IT staff and designated managers with badge and biometric authentication.
  • 24/7 Access: Data center is accessible only to authorized personnel at all times; all entries/exits are logged.
  • Vendor Access: Vendors and non-employees require pre-approval and must be escorted by IT at all times.
  • Cabinet Security: Server racks are kept locked at all times. Keys held only by IT staff.
  • Emergency Access: Emergency entry is permitted for authorized staff only and must be logged with the on-call manager.

Removable Media Policy

  • Approved Devices Only: Only company-issued, encrypted USB drives may be used. Personal USBs are strictly prohibited.
  • Enforcement: USB ports are disabled by default; exceptions require IT approval. Regular audits are performed.
  • If in Doubt: Never plug in an unknown USB device. Report it to IT immediately. Violations may lead to disciplinary action (and disappointed IT staff).

For any questions about this policy, please contact the NerdHerd IT department. Thank you for keeping our office and data safe!
